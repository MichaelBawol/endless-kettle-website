Our Story

Born during a storm, built with heart

The Origin

A Bold Leap of Faith

The Endless Kettle was founded in 2020 by Michael, right in the midst of the Covid pandemic. While the world was shutting down, Michael was opening up — turning a bold dream into reality.

What started as a leap of faith has grown into a beloved neighbourhood gathering place. A space where the community comes together over great food, warm hospitality, and the kind of atmosphere that feels like home.

The Space

A Beautiful Victorian Bank

We moved into our beautiful old Victorian bank at 61 North Street in Keighley and fell in love with it. The character, the history, the grandeur — it was the perfect home for The Endless Kettle.

We spent over four months lovingly restoring the main dining room, paying special attention to the stunning historic ceiling details that give our space its unique character. If you are an art-deco lover, you will truly appreciate it.

The Kitchen

A Kitchen That Transforms

Our kitchen transforms throughout the day. By day, we serve classic English fare with a modern twist and a touch of Polish influence — hearty breakfasts and satisfying lunches crafted with care.

By evening, the kitchen shifts to Korean cuisine with Japanese influence — bold flavours, vibrant dishes, and our legendary Korean Fried Chicken. Our menus are carefully curated to offer something for all tastes and dietary needs.

The Heart

Where It All Comes Together

At the heart of The Endless Kettle is our in-house bakery, where Michael's mother Saskia creates the most amazing cakes, pastries, and cheesecakes you will find anywhere.

Sasa brings creativity, warmth, and a deeply personal touch to everything she bakes. Her work is the creative and personal foundation of our business — a labour of love that you can taste in every bite.
